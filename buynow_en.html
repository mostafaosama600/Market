Define the challenge when buying

A good place to start when considering the type of designer you need is to look at the scale and nature of the challenge facing your business. Consider any potential implications and impacts a new design initiative could have on other parts of the business at this point, and make sure the right people are involved in the starting conversations.

At this early stage, you haven't yet tried to determine the final design output in terms of a brochure, website, or service, but you're thinking broadly about what kind of help you need.

Brief development

The next step in beginning to clarify your requirements is to draft a summary (detailed in Section 2). All designers will want to start from a similar point. They will need some information about your business background and the nature of the project as well as what you will need from them.

The summary is a starting point for internal communication and discussion as well as a tool you can use when searching for and selecting designers, thus ensuring everyone is on the same page.
